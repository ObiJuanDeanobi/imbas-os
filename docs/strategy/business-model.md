# Imbas OS business model

This is a working strategy note, not a public pricing page.

## Principle

Imbas OS should earn trust by making the local-first core real.

The free/open-source core should be useful on its own. Paid offerings should charge for convenience, hosted reliability, collaboration, compliance, and business risk reduction — not for crippling basic local ownership.

## Recommended model

```text
Free/open-source local core
  → community adoption, trust, self-hosting, agent-builder credibility

Patreon / supporter early access
  → funds development, gives power users a feedback loop, previews experimental work

Paid cloud/workspace product
  → hosted sync, backups, collaboration, admin/compliance, managed connectors
```

## Free/open-source core

The local core should include:

- desktop workbench;
- local vault/artifacts/wiki;
- Runledger;
- Lorekeeper proposals and guarded review flows;
- basic Conduit APIs;
- local context packs;
- basic Android companion pairing/use;
- self-hostable/private-first workflows;
- clear docs and verification gates.

The core promise:

> Imbas OS is free and local-first. You own your agent memory, artifacts, wiki, and run history.

## Patreon / supporter early access

Patreon is for supporters and early adopters, not teams/business operations.

Use US dollars for supporter tiers.

Suggested tiers:

- **$5/mo — Supporter**
  - development updates;
  - supporter badge/credit where appropriate;
  - roadmap visibility.

- **$12/mo — Early Access**
  - preview builds;
  - experimental feature access;
  - feedback channel;
  - early docs/demo drops.

- **$25/mo — Founder**
  - founder credit/badge;
  - priority feedback consideration;
  - occasional private demo/build notes;
  - influence on early roadmap direction.

Avoid promising guaranteed custom work, SLAs, or team support through Patreon. That belongs to the paid cloud/workspace product.

Suggested framing:

> Imbas OS is free and local-first. Patreon supports development and gives early access to experimental builds. Paid cloud/workspace plans fund hosted infrastructure, team workflows, and business-grade controls.

## Paid cloud/workspace product

This should be separate from Patreon and treated almost like its own product line.

Potential paid features:

- hosted sync/backup;
- browser-accessible cloud workspace;
- managed relay for mobile/remote agents;
- team/org workspaces;
- collaboration/review queues;
- admin controls;
- SSO/SAML later;
- audit retention and exports;
- compliance evidence packs;
- hosted connector infrastructure;
- managed OpenClaw/agent runner options;
- priority support and deployment help.

The paid business pitch:

> Pay when you want hosted sync, managed backups, team collaboration, compliance-grade auditability, and business controls.

## What not to do

- Do not make basic local memory/artifact ownership paid-only.
- Do not blur Patreon with team/business licensing.
- Do not promise business SLAs through supporter tiers.
- Do not pitch public 1.0 until the fresh-system and documentation gates pass.
- Do not lead marketing with internal subsystem names before the user understands the problem.
